# Mental Awareness Chatbot Training Trees

# TREE 1: OVERTHINKING

Root Question: I feel like I'm constantly overthinking everything. How
do I stop this?

Response: Overthinking happens when the mind repeatedly tries to solve
or predict outcomes. Slowing down thoughts through grounding, writing,
or focusing on the present can help break the cycle.

Follow-up 1

Question: What situations trigger this feeling? Answer: Understanding
triggers can help identify patterns.

Follow-up 2

Question: How long have you been feeling this way? Answer: Duration
helps understand whether it is temporary or ongoing.

Follow-up 3

Question: Do these thoughts affect your daily activities? Answer: Strong
emotions can interfere with concentration or productivity.

Follow-up 4

Question: Do you notice physical symptoms like tension or headaches?
Answer: Mental stress often appears in physical form.

Follow-up 5

Question: Have you talked to someone you trust about this? Answer:
Sharing experiences can reduce emotional pressure.

Follow-up 6

Question: What usually helps you feel a little calmer? Answer:
Recognizing helpful activities builds coping strategies.

Follow-up 7

Question: Would you like to talk more about what's been happening
recently? Answer: Exploring recent events may reveal possible causes.

BRANCH: Emotional Support

Trigger: Stress / Emotion / Personal Concern

Response: These feelings are common human experiences and talking about
them can often help bring clarity and relief.

# TREE 21: JOB STRESS

Root Question:
My job is stressing me out.

Response:
Work stress can happen when responsibilities, deadlines, or expectations start feeling overwhelming. When stress builds up over time, it can affect both mental and emotional well-being.

Follow-up 1

Question: What part of your job feels the most stressful right now?
Answer: Identifying the specific source of stress can help determine whether the issue comes from workload, deadlines, or workplace relationships.

Follow-up 2

Question: Do you feel overwhelmed by the number of tasks you have?
Answer: Having too many responsibilities at once can make it difficult to stay focused and productive.

Follow-up 3

Question: Do you feel supported by your coworkers or manager?
Answer: Supportive coworkers and managers can make stressful situations easier to handle.

Follow-up 4

Question: Are you able to disconnect from work after office hours?
Answer: Taking time away from work mentally helps prevent long-term stress and burnout.

Follow-up 5

Question: Do you feel pressure to perform perfectly at work?
Answer: Perfectionism can create unnecessary stress and increase fear of making mistakes.

Follow-up 6

Question: Have you been taking regular breaks during work?
Answer: Short breaks during the day help the brain recharge and improve productivity.

Follow-up 7

Question: Do you feel your work-life balance is healthy?
Answer: Maintaining a balance between work responsibilities and personal life is important for mental health.

BRANCH: Workload Pressure

Trigger: Workload / Deadlines / Pressure

Response:
Heavy workloads and tight deadlines can create mental pressure and increase stress levels.

Follow-up:
Do you feel like your workload has increased recently?

Answer:
Sudden increases in responsibilities can make work feel overwhelming and difficult to manage.
